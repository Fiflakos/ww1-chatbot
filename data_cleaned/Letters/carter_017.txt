Author: Herbert Gordon Carter Date: 5 May 1915 Nationality: Australian Text: 5th May, 1915My Dear Father & MotherThings are a bit milder in the trenchesbut nevertheless the war game is not what its cracked upto be. - - It's pretty easy to hear of a lot of one'sfriends getting knocked out - You will have seen thecasualty list by now. I don't mind the hardships abit. Just now we are sleeping - or trying to sleep -on the edge of the mountains; if we roll in our sleepwe may roll farther than bargained. I have nothad my clothes off for nearly a week. - I'venow got used to noise - can sleep most soundleythro' a rattling fusillade
